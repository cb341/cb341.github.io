Custom GPU raymarcher implemented in GLSL within Three.js. Instead of analytic signed distance functions, voxel density is sampled from 3D textures and traversed via adaptive field stepping.

Initial implementations relied on small fixed steps and suffered from excessive texture sampling per pixel. To improve performance, I precomputed a higher resolution 3D texture using a CPU side Euclidean distance propagation algorithm to determine maximum safe jump distances.

This discrete distance volume enabled adaptive stepping and improved performance by roughly an order of magnitude. Remaining stair stepping artifacts near surfaces highlighted discretization limits and minimum step constraints inherent to voxel based distance fields.
